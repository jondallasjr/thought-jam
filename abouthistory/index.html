Our History

From a back-room book club to a global movement. This is the story of how curiosity, persistence, and community transformed philosophy from inaccessible to irresistible.

Where It All Began

March 3, 2017. A small group gathered in the back room of a Hanoi cafe for what would become the first Hanoi Philosophy Forum event. No one knew it then, but this humble book club would evolve into Thought Jam - a global movement that has hosted 239 events for 8,488 guests across 11 cities.

Back then, the project was simple: a free philosophy book club where volunteers explored the writings of conventional academic philosophers like Plato, Nietzsche, Lao Tzu, and others. Everyone involved was a volunteer. There were no fancy presentations, no multimedia elements, no themed drinks. Just curious minds trying to make sense of big ideas.

But it was a start. And from that modest beginning, something remarkable began to grow.

The Evolution

March 2017

The First Event

The Hanoi Philosophy Forum hosted its first event on March 3, 2017. A philosophy book club focused on conventional academic thinkers. Free admission. All volunteers. Pure experimentation.

April 2017

The Accessibility Problem

Within weeks, we discovered a critical issue: most attendees arrived without completing the suggested readings. They were unprepared, confused, and the conversations suffered. Something had to change.

May 2017

Introducing Lectures

We began preparing and delivering presentations that broke down complex philosophical concepts, offering accessible contextualization. Guests loved it - they could ask questions, and they appreciated that presenters were learners, not experts. We explored Michel Foucault, Zhuangzi, Carl Jung, Arthur Schopenhauer, Simone de Beauvoir, and Noam Chomsky.

July 2017

New Venue, New Technology

Outgrew our back room at Clickspace and moved to Bluebirds' Nest. For the first time, we had real technology: projector, projection screen, microphone, PA system. The experience became more professional, more polished.

2018

Pivot to Contemporary Issues

We shifted from conventional philosophy to divisive contemporary issues. Veganism. Nationalism. Immigration. Gender identity. Suddenly, philosophy wasn't just about dead thinkers - it was about the conflicts tearing society apart right now.

2019

Multimedia & Multifaceted

Events evolved into multimedia experiences. Live music. Interactive games. Commissioned artwork. Panel discussions with experts. We weren't just teaching philosophy - we were entertaining while we educated.

2020-2021

Going Global

Expanded beyond Hanoi to Chiang Mai, Singapore, Da Nang, Bali, and online. The pandemic forced adaptation, but it also proved our model worked anywhere. Philosophy without borders.

2023-2024

Africa & Beyond

Launched in Harare, Zimbabwe, bringing our approach to new continents. Real Talk Philosophy became Thought Jam, reflecting our evolution from philosophy forum to cultural movement.

2025

Today

239 events. 8,488 guests. 11 cities across 3 continents. From a back-room book club to the most entertaining way to explore divisive issues. The journey continues.
